# Brazil Lane Validation + Global Analogs (2026-06-07)

**Milestone: the strongest validation yet. Brazil is a genuine open lane for a capital-light multi-FI moto-loan-origination front-end, the aggregator shape is right (integration *failed* there), FI supply is deeply panelable, and global precedents give a precise blueprint. The one remaining unknown is the same one: cheap, defensible buyer acquisition.**

## Brazil — validated on every axis

| Test | Result |
|---|---|
| Galgo-free? | ✅ Yes — Galgo only in CL/PE/CO/MX; no BR entity/plan/hiring |
| Multi-FI moto-*purchase* front-end exists? | ✅ No — genuine gap |
| Panel multiple FIs? | ✅ Yes, deeply (dealer-led market, open correspondent programs) |
| Integrated model viable in BR? | ⚠️ Already failed — Creditas/**Voltz** collapsed (judicial recovery, R$335M debt) → argues for aggregator, not integration |

**Competitive structure:** the true multi-lender marketplaces (FinanZero 70+ FIs, Juros Baixos 40+) do moto-as-*collateral* refi only, **not purchase**. The moto-*purchase* platforms (Mobiauto→PAN, Webmotors→Santander, iCarros→Itaú, NaPista→BV, Banco Honda captive) are all **single-bank captive** — none compares multiple FIs for the same bike. **Closest integrator threat = Mottu** (owns demand+credit+sale+insurance, but only for captive delivery riders on its own fleet — ~1 strategic pivot from going broad). **Banco PAN** = ~35% moto-finance share but pure FI (owns credit, dealer-dependent for demand; owns Mobiauto). Secondary risk: June-2026 BNDES delivery-rider credit line.

**Market:** Honda's finance arm captured 47% of its moto sales — but **~32pp consórcio + ~15pp Banco Honda CDC** (true loan penetration ~15%); moto financing +10–18% in 2025 while cars stalled (motos carry the whole auto-credit market); **new motos dominate financing (B3 Nov 2025: 116k new vs 46k used)** — used is the *underserved minority* (~28% of contracts). *(Corrected 2026-06-10: earlier version inverted the new/used split and conflated consórcio with loans.)*

**The crux (unchanged):** the gap is on the **demand side**, not the matching tech (PAN owns the dealer rails). Cheapest defensible acquisition = **NOT Google CPC** (bid up by banks + content farms) but **dealer-embed at independent moto lojas** (neutral multi-FI simulator; pay the lojista the same embedded commission) + **WhatsApp** + **used-moto long-tail SEO**.

## Brazil FI panel (supply side — deep & open)

Launch panel spanning the full risk spectrum, all open programs:
1. **Omni** — moto *specialist*, best for thin-file/subprime/autônomos (the true moto buyer). **The anchor.**
2. **Banco PAN** — explicit C/D/E focus, 20k-store lojista program.
3. **BV** — broad mid-risk auto/moto book, simple online correspondent signup.
4. **Santander Financiamentos** — prime, 100% LTV, "Seja um Afiliado" referral.
5. **Consórcio (Servopa / Rodobens)** — for credit-fails (no-interest fallback).

Commission ~1.2–1.8% of financed (CMN cap 6%); consórcio recurring. **Certification gate:** FEBRABAN FBB-100/130 for CDC correspondents. **Exclude:** Crefisa (collateral refi only — wrong product), Mycon (direct-only), Banco Honda (captive). Creditas = easy API but prime-skewed (keep for higher scores).

## Global analogs — the blueprint

| Player | Region | Model | Lesson |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Rupyy** (CarDekho) | India | multi-lender (37+) + small NBFC; owns BikeDekho audience | capital-light scales (+88% YoY) — but 91% used-car; **2W alone too thin** |
| **Paisabazaar** | India | pure aggregator, multi-lender | profitable at scale; **RBI unsecured crackdown = regulatory tail risk** |
| **OTO Capital** | India | balance-sheet 2W | **bled cash (−₹30Cr)** — don't hold the book |
| **Moxa (Astra)** | Indonesia | multi-lender super-app | works but walled-garden (one group) |
| **Watu / Mogo** | Africa | balance-sheet integrator | **Watu profit −85% on defaults**; predatory-lending probes = reputation risk |
| **M-KOPA** | Kenya | integrator w/ walk-away/refund + Bolt partnership | **trust design + partner-for-demand** is the counter-model |
| **MAX.ng** | Nigeria | own ride-app feeds drivers | **owning rider demand → near-zero churn / cheap CAC** |
| **CarsDirect** | US | pure subprime lead vendor (soft-pull pre-screen) | the clean capital-light front-end |
| **RouteOne / Dealertrack** | US | multi-lender origination rails | the matching-layer precedent (one app → many lenders) |
| **Capital One Auto Navigator** | US | soft-pull prequal marketplace | **closest single analog** — demand-gen + soft-pull + matching UX |

**Top lessons (the playbook):**
1. **Be the front-end + matching layer, never the balance sheet** (US split lead-vendor/rails from risk; Watu/OTO/Voltz all bled holding the book).
2. **Soft-pull pre-qualification is the unlock** (Capital One Navigator) — pre-screen, then fan one app across the FI panel (RouteOne mechanic).
3. **Attach a basket** — 2W is the wedge; monetize insurance + refi + used + personal loans (Rupyy/Paisabazaar).
4. **Own/partner-for rider demand** to crush CAC (MAX, M-KOPA×Bolt); don't buy cold leads.
5. **Trust/transparency = compliance moat** (M-KOPA, not Watu).

## Bottom line

Brazil is the lane; the aggregator shape is validated (integration failed there); FI supply is panelable; the blueprint is precise (capital-light front-end + soft-pull pre-qual + multi-FI matching + attached cross-sell + dealer-embed acquisition + trust positioning). **Remaining unknown = the same crux: can you acquire financeable moto buyers cheaply via dealer-embed/WhatsApp/used-SEO before Mottu pivots or a captive reacts?** Now a concrete Phase-1/2 test, not an open mystery.

## Caveats

Galgo "Brazil-absent" = no public evidence (confirm before betting). Competitor finance-product details (who does *purchase* vs *collateral*) are high-confidence; exact FI commission %s are market-norm/secondary. Global analog outcomes are press/secondary. Mottu-pivot risk is judgment.
