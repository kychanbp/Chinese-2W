A. 3 Most Important Errors

  1. Claim 10 overstates or mixes challenger growth: Hero Colombia looks closer to +116%, JCH Peru closer to +18.5%, and Avelloz +82% was not verified.
  2. Claim 12 is the weakest: it reads like precise unit economics, but the evidence is mostly extrapolated/vendor/blog-level, not LatAm motorcycle dealer audited data.
  3. Claim 1 mixes definitions: HS 8711 value and “motorcycle industry” unit/value sources are not the same universe.

B. Materially Overstated / Unsupported

The dealer CAC model is directionally useful but should not be treated as proven. The strongest support is for “fast WhatsApp response helps” and “vehicle-finance correspondents earn commissions,” not the exact CPL/conversion/organic percentages.

C. Overall

Directionally trustworthy on market growth, brand concentration, Brazil/Shineray, Mexico/Italika, Colombia/Peru structure, and the China-direct challenger thesis. Less trustworthy on precise challenger growth rates outside Brazil and on dealer CAC/unit-economics inputs.
