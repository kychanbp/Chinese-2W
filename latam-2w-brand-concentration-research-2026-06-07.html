LatAm Motorcycle Brand Concentration (2026-06-07)

Why this matters: the lead-gen thesis leaned on an assumption that LatAm 2W markets are "fragmented (multi-brand)." A 4-agent brand-share sweep tested it. Verdict: the assumption is wrong for the two biggest markets — they're brand-concentrated — but the "challengers pushing hard to enter" half is strongly confirmed, which is what actually matters for the brand-customer thesis.

Pattern: the biggest markets (BR, MX) are concentrated; the fragmented markets (Peru, Argentina, DR) are smaller. So "a fragmented big market" does not exist in LatAm 2W.

Brazil — concentrated top, fragmented tail (2025 Fenabrave)

Honda 66.8% (1.47M), Yamaha 14.2% (311k), Shineray 5.9% (130.6k, China), Mottu ~4.5% (fleet/rental, TVS-design), Avelloz ~1.5%, Royal Enfield, Bajaj, Haojue ~1.0%. Top-3 = 87%. Honda eroding (>75% in 2022 → 67% in 2025). The sub-3% tail is volatile with triple-digit-growth challengers.

Mexico — Italika monopoly-ish (2024)

Italika ~69% (1.2–1.3M units), then Vento, Honda, Bajaj, Yamaha each low-single-digit; local/assembled brands >85% of sales. Italika's moat is distribution, not product — Zongshen engines, sold + financed via Elektra/Banco Azteca to under-banked buyers. 2026 import tariff ~35% (not ~25% — corrected) on non-FTA (China) bikes rewards local assembly.

Colombia & Peru

  • Colombia (concentrated): Yamaha ~19%, AKT ~16% (Corbeta; Loncin/QJ/SYM CKD), Bajaj ~14%, Suzuki ~12%, Honda ~11%. Top-5 ≈ 70%. Chinese product laundered behind AKT / Victory (Auteco; Zontes).
  • Peru (fragmented): Honda ~24%, Bajaj 9.3%, then a Chinese single-digit tail — Ronco 8.9%, Wanxin 8.7%, Zongshen 3.8%, JCH, Ssenda, Nexus, Lifan. 200+ brands. Chinese-origin = clear plurality, direct badges (not laundered).

Argentina / Venezuela / Dominican Republic

  • Argentina (fragmented): Honda #1 but <10%; house brands Motomel, Corven, Gilera, Keller, Zanella — all heavily Chinese-CKD-sourced (laundered).
  • Venezuela (concentrated, Chinese): >90% of sales Chinese; Bera + Empire Keeway (Keeway/QJ) dominate via local assembly. 2024 imports ~$355M from China (~96%), +$167M YoY — corroborates the "units doubled" surge. Cash/informal, low financing.
  • Dominican Rep. (fragmented): India (Bajaj/AKT/TVS/Hero) + Japan (Honda/Yamaha/Suzuki) split; CFMoto/Zontes/Kove entering fast.

The challenger cohort (the real brand-customers)

The "pushing hard to enter" finding is the decision-relevant one — these demand-gen-starved entrants are who the brand/lead layers serve:

  • Shineray — Brazil #3, +93% (2024), self-distributes 438 stores. The breakout.
  • CFMoto, QJ Motor, Zontes, Voge, Kove — scaling across MX/CO/DR.
  • Wanxin, Lifan, JCH, Zongshen — fragmented Peru.
  • Incumbents are China-sourced underneath (Italika=Zongshen; AKT/Victory=Loncin/QJ/Zontes).

Implication for the lead-gen thesis

  1. Drop "fragmented market" as the framing — false for BR/MX at the brand level; Honda/Italika are non-customers and non-displaceable.
  2. The opportunity = (a) the named challenger brands (Shineray, CFMoto, QJ, Zontes, Voge…) fighting for the tail, and (b) the genuinely fragmented smaller markets (Peru, Argentina, DR).
  3. Beachhead = a challenger's push inside a big market (ride Shineray's BR expansion) rather than "a fragmented big market," which doesn't exist.
  4. Brand concentration ≠ funnel capture — the challenger tail's funnel is still open (the open-vs-captured map holds for the tail).

Confidence & caveats

High on Brazil/Mexico rankings (Fenabrave / MotorcyclesData / Motofichas); medium on sub-top share %s (press gives ordering, not clean splits); medium-low on Venezuela/DR unit shares (no clean official tables — directional, corroborated by trade-value data). Mexico 2026 tariff corrected to ~35% (was ~25% in prior notes).
