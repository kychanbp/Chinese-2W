# LatAm Challenger Moto Brands — Growth + Demand-Gen Fit (2024–25)

**Purpose:** identify the brand-layer customer pipeline. The filter is two-part: **(1) fast-growing** (pushing to enter/gain share) AND **(2) third-party-distributed with weak in-house marketing** (would *outsource* demand-gen) — NOT self-distributors who build their own funnel.

## The key finding

The **fastest-growing challenger in the biggest market — Shineray (Brazil) — self-distributes (438 own stores) and is therefore NOT a demand-gen customer.** The breakout builds in-house. **But** a recurring cohort of **China-direct, mid-cc, third-party-importer brands — CFMoto, Zontes, QJ Motor, Voge, Kove — appears across MX/CO/AR/DR** and *would* outsource. Add India challengers (Bajaj, Hero, TVS) and small-market house brands (Peru JCH/Nexus/Ssenda, AR Keller, VE Toro). **The pipeline is real but specific — not "Chinese brands" broadly.**

## The brand-customer shortlist (cross-market)

1. **China-direct mid-cc cohort — CFMoto, Zontes, QJ Motor, Voge, Kove** (recur in MX/CO/AR/DR; dealer/importer-distributed; tariff-pressured → need volume).
2. **India challengers — Bajaj (BR), Hero (CO/MX), TVS (CO/MX/DR)** (foreign brands building local awareness via 3rd-party channels).
3. **Fragmented-market house brands — JCH/Nexus/Ssenda (PE), Keller (AR), Toro (VE)** (Chinese-sourced, retail-distributed, no in-house marketing).

**Avoid (self-distribute / captive):** Shineray (438 stores), Mottu (rental fleet), Royal Enfield (premium), incumbents (Honda/Italika/Bera/Empire Keeway/Motomel/Gilera).

## Implication

The brand-layer thesis survives but narrows: target the **3rd-party-distributed challengers**, lead with the **recurring China-direct cohort** (one pitch, many markets) and the **India challengers**. The Mexico 2026 ~35% China tariff is a tailwind — it pressures exactly these brands to defend share, raising the value of demand-gen. **Open risk to validate in Phase 1: do these specific brands actually outsource demand-gen, or replicate Shineray's self-distribution once they scale?**

## Confidence & caveats

BR/MX/CO/AR rankings are hard-sourced (Fenabrave / MotorcyclesData / ACARA / Andemos); growth %s often mix H1/YTD vs full-year (flagged) — treat ranks as reliable, magnitudes as directional.
