# Comparables Teardown — the 3 closest models to copy

No exact twin exists (that's the gap). The build is a **composite of three**: **Rupyy** (the engine + acquisition), **FinanZero** (the Brazilian license + structure), **Capital One Auto Navigator** (the prequal UX). Take-rate / CAC are mostly *not disclosed* by any of them — flagged.

## At a glance

| | **Rupyy** (CarDekho, IN) | **FinanZero** (BR) | **Cap One Auto Navigator** (US) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Model | Multi-lender marketplace + thin own-NBFC | **Pure broker, zero balance sheet** | **Single-lender** (Cap One's own book) |
| Panel | **37+ lenders** (25+ for 2W) | **~50–60 FIs** | 1 (Capital One) |
| Monetization | commission/disbursal (~1–2.5%, est.) + insurance attach | success fee per closed loan (~2–8% BR norm, est.) | own loan interest |
| Acquisition | **dealer point-of-sale QR** (digiQode) — the cheap high-intent channel | **SEO** (5th most-searched credit brand in BR) + media-for-equity TV | soft-pull prequal + inventory browse |
| Moto/purchase? | 2W flow exists but car-skewed (91% used-car) | **CDC moto-purchase already in funnel**, but thin | auto only |
| Scale | **$2B ARR**, ~15% used-car-loan share | R$900M facilitated, 31M DB, 3M visits/mo | 13,000+ dealers; ~95% prequal→sale (one dealer) |
| The one lesson | **win buyers at the dealer POS, not via ads** | **correspondente bancário = capital-light unlock** | **soft-pull → real personalized installment** |

## Rupyy (CarDekho) — the engine + acquisition

**Model:** multi-lender loan-origination platform (deep API integration to **37+ banks/NBFCs**; 25+ on the 2W flow) earning a **distributor commission per disbursal** — capital-light, FI carries risk. A *thin* own-NBFC sleeve (CarDekho Financial Services) fills gaps + dealer floor-plan; not the core.

**Acquisition (the key finding):** despite owning CarDekho/BikeDekho's huge organic audience, Rupyy's *dominant, cheapest* channel is the **in-dealership QR code** (digiQode/digiPartner) at the *moment of purchase* — video-KYC + e-NACH + e-sign → **sanction at POS in minutes**, disbursal ≤48h. ~**14,000 distributors, 1,500 cities, 95%+ of pincodes**. CAC ≈ the dealer-partner relationship, not ad spend.

**Scale:** **~$2B ARR (FY24)**, **~15% used-car-loan market share** (largest distributor), ~₹16,000 Cr disbursed FY25, new-car financing +97% YoY. Heavily car-skewed (used-car = 91% of FY23 revenue); 2W is small/newer. CarDekho committed ~$100M into Rupyy.

**Copy:** (1) lead with **dealer-POS QR**, not ads; (2) capital-light brokered + thin NBFC for gaps only; (3) one application → broad panel via a *real multi-lender LOS* (the moat is the integration, not a lead form); (4) **win one vertical deeply** (moto) before fanning out — Rupyy owned used-car first; (5) compress to **instant** (digital KYC + e-mandate + e-sign at POS → Brazil equivalents: Open Finance, Pix automático, gov.br/ICP sign).

## FinanZero — the Brazilian license + structure

**Model:** **pure broker / marketplace, zero balance sheet, zero credit risk.** One consumer application → shopped across **~50–60 partner FIs** → **up to 10 pre-approved offers in ~5 min**, money in 48h. **Soft-pull** (only ID + address + income up front). Free to consumer, 100% digital. Approval rate **~10%** → top-funnel volume is everything (**92% of apps start on mobile**).

**Regulatory unlock:** operates as a **correspondente bancário** (CMN Res. 4.935/2021, which replaced Res. 3.954/11) — the legal vehicle that lets a non-bank intermediate credit and earn commission while the FI underwrites/disburses/holds the loan. **This is the capital-light license to replicate.**

**Products:** personal/unsecured (core), vehicle-collateral refi, consignado, **and vehicle-PURCHASE CDC** — FinanZero explicitly markets CDC for *acquiring motorcycles*. So **moto-purchase is already a node in the funnel — just thin and consumer-pull (no dealer channel).** That's the white space.

**Acquisition:** **SEO/content is the moat** — FinanZero is the **5th most-searched credit brand in Brazil** (~3M visits/mo, ~1.5M apps/mo in 2022). Plus a **$4M media-for-equity TV deal (4Equity)** → ~25% lower effective CAC. (Exact R$ CAC not disclosed.)

**Scale/funding:** ~R$900M facilitated (2022, cumulative), 31M database, ~52 staff; founded 2016 (Swedes/Webrock), ~$27–33M raised, ~$67.8M valuation (2021); VEF, Dunross, 4Equity. *(Juros Baixos = same model, ~20–40 partners, pivoted to embedded-finance/CaaS API.)*

**Port to moto-purchase:** (1) the **correspondente license is the unlock**, not a balance sheet; (2) CDC-moto is proven but thin in their consumer-pull funnel → **dealer point-of-sale integration is the white space**; (3) SEO is durable but slow → **out-niche on moto intent** ("financiamento de moto sem entrada"), don't fight generic "empréstimo"; (4) one-app → multiple soft-pull offers in 5 min is the conversion mechanic (need ~30+ FIs to be credible); (5) **media-for-equity** to buy TV/brand without cash CAC.

## Capital One Auto Navigator — the prequal UX

**Model:** **single-lender** (Capital One's own book) **soft-pull prequal + inventory-browse** marketplace across 13,000+ dealers. *Not* a multi-lender aggregator — note clearly.

**The soft-pull mechanic (the thing to copy):** a **soft credit pull, no score impact**; buyer enters thin data (name, address, DOB, **stated** income, employment); within minutes sees a **real personalized APR / term / monthly payment**, and flexes down-payment/trade-in/term with the payment recomputing live. **Conditional, non-binding** — confirmed at the hard pull at the dealer; offers expire ~30 days.

**Funnel:** prequal *first* (soft) → **browse inventory with "your" monthly payment on every VIN** → configure per car → take prequal to dealer → hard pull + paperwork at the store. Financing is pulled to the *top* of the funnel; the dealer becomes fulfillment. One dealer reported **~95% of Cap-One-prequalified buyers leave with a car.**

**Why it works:** removes the hard-pull-before-shopping fear; anchors on a *real personalized payment* (affordability, not sticker price); pre-sorted high-intent leads raise dealer close rates (which funds the free-to-consumer model).

**Copy / adapt for Brazil moto:** (1) **soft-pull-first, browse-second** sequencing (in BR, Serasa/Boa Vista *consultas* don't ding scores like US hard pulls — the "no impact" promise is *easier* to keep); (2) show a **real personalized installment**, not a generic calculator; (3) **thin-file won't translate** — sub-bancarizado moto buyers need **alternative-data prequal** (telco, CadÚnico, **PIX cash-flow**, device, prior installments) or the funnel-top is empty; (4) keep prequal conditional but make verification **lightweight** (Open-Finance PIX/payroll pull) to shrink the prequal→approval gap; (5) **reconsider single-lender** → a **multi-lender cascading waterfall** (prime bank → consignado → fintech → consórcio fallback) salvages declines a single book rejects — approval rate matters far more in thin-file Brazil than in the US.

## Synthesis — the Brazil moto blueprint (composite)

> **FinanZero's license + Rupyy's dealer-POS engine + Capital One's prequal UX — multi-lender, moto-first, in Brazil.**

- **Legal/structure:** capital-light **correspondente bancário** broker (FinanZero) — no balance sheet, commission per disbursal.
- **Credit core:** **multi-lender cascading waterfall** (Rupyy's deep-LOS panel; the Omni/PAN/BV/Santander/consórcio set) with **alternative-data soft-pull prequal** for thin-file moto buyers (Cap One UX + BR alt-data).
- **Acquisition:** **dealer-POS QR at independent moto lojas** (Rupyy's cheap high-intent channel — the white space FinanZero never took) + **moto-niche SEO** + media-for-equity for brand. *Not* Google CPC.
- **UX:** soft-pull → **"your R$X/month" on every bike** → instant digital KYC/e-sign → sanction at POS.
- **Edge vs Galgo:** capital-light **multi-FI** (higher approval via waterfall + capital efficiency) vs Galgo's single balance sheet; defensible in **Galgo-free Brazil**.
- **The crux it still rides on:** can the **dealer-POS + moto-SEO** acquisition deliver financeable buyers cheaply — the one number Phase-1/2 must measure.

## Caveats

Take-rates and R$ CAC are **not publicly disclosed** by Rupyy or FinanZero (industry-norm inferences flagged). FinanZero scale figures are 2022 (no 2024–25 update found). Capital One's soft-pull doctrine is US-bureau-specific; the Brazil translation (alt-data) is the unproven adaptation. The ~95% prequal→sale is one dealer's anecdote.
