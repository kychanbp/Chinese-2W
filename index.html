Bottom line up front

Verdict

The opportunity

The bet — ride an accelerating wave into its best-shaped market

Export size alone isn't opportunity — growth is. A big-but-flat number could mean a saturated market; what makes this a bet is that the wave is re-accelerating and the markets under it are growing. China's 2W exports were flat-to-down in 2022–23 (~$11.6B), then jumped to $14.5B / 36.75M units in 2024, and LatAm is the fastest-growing major destination — Mexico #2 worldwide, Venezuela the standout surge (#6, units doubled YoY).

The shape of the thing

The model — an asset-light lead factory

A social-to-WhatsApp engine that manufactures finance-ready buyers and hands them to sellers:

TikTok / FB / IG content + ads→ click-to-WhatsApp→ qualify (intent + financeable)→ hand off to dealer / pre-approve a loan

WhatsApp is on ~99% of Brazilian phones; click-to-WhatsApp slashes cost-per-lead. It's all Chinese 2W (ICE + EV), not EV-only — the lead-gen wedge doesn't care about the powertrain, it cares about demand-gen-starved sellers and financeable buyers.

What the numbers actually say

The hard truth — the dealer-only model is marginal

Grounded in a 4-agent dealer-CAC research sweep, the per-lead economics are unforgiving. The chain of why:

ForceWhat it does
Price ceiling = min(ROI limit, dealer's own CAC)A dealer pays at most what it costs them to get the buyer themselves. That ceiling is just 0.30% of the bike price → a hard price floor ~$1,667 below which dealer-only can't work.
Arbitrage compressionYou and the dealer buy from the same Meta auction (~$5/lead each) → the pure per-lead spread is near zero.

This isn't pessimism — it's what the model returns when you plug in real LatAm ad costs, the dealer's self-serve alternative, and organic share. The naive version fails on arithmetic, not execution. Why it isn't dead

Why it works — one buyer, three payers

The model survives because a single acquired buyer is sold, in different forms, to three different payers — and two of the three are not capped by the dealer's ROI hurdle. This is aggregator economics: you own the scarce asset (the buyer relationship) and tax every party that needs access.

BRAND → demand
$1–3k/mo retainer + market-entry + data. New entrants pay above any hurdle (market-entry budget). Stickiest, non-arbitraged.
DEALER → the buyer
$4–8/lead, capped & arbitraged. Role: cover CAC — not where the profit is.
LENDER → the loan
$45–175/loan origination (2–5%) + insurance. Paid from interest margin → uncapped. The real moat.

Financing flips the unit economics positive (dealer-only CM −$0.10 → with financing positive; premium bike + dual stream → ~+$5,286/mo). The brand retainer escapes the ceiling entirely. Catch: financing normally belongs to the dealer's F&I desk — capture it by pre-approving the buyer upstream, before the handoff.

The targeting that makes or breaks it

Who it's for — and the constraint that binds

The model only works for a deliberately narrow customer, and fails by design for everyone else:

✓ Your ICP
Organic-poor, demand-gen-starved, weak-F&I dealers and new Chinese brands with no awareness yet. They have no buyer pull and can't self-serve well → they need you, and you can capture financing they don't.
✗ Not your customer
Strong incumbents (good ads + organic + F&I). Their make-cost ≤ your CAC (won't pay), and their brand pull captures buyers before they enter your funnel.
The binding constraint — buyer interception. The whole model lives on catching the buyer in the open social/WhatsApp channel before a dealer's organic pull does. Where incumbents are strong (Honda/Yamaha walk-ins), the buyer is gone before you can touch them. Your funnel exists only where there's no pull — new brands nobody searches yet, no-brand fragmented dealers, open-channel comparison shoppers.

What keeps it from being copied

Defensibility — beyond the arbitraged layer
Finance-qualification
Collecting income/credit-consent in the WhatsApp flow makes the buyer bankable — the asset a plain lead lacks, and the key to capturing loan origination upstream.
Conversion-data = pricing power
You measure conversion (buyer follow-up + finance rail), so you anchor the price. Cross-dealer comparison separates lead quality from closing skill — the dealer can't blame your leads.
Speed-to-lead
Sub-minute WhatsApp reply converts ~20% vs ~6% slow (decays to ~1% at 24h). An operational moat a busy dealer can't match.
Organic content / SEO + LTV
Owned free traffic compounds; keeping the buyer's WhatsApp relationship unlocks renewals, upgrades, refinance, referrals — the only non-arbitraged, recurring layers.

What we still don't know

The crux → a $3–5k, 90-day test

Three things decide the business and none can be settled from a desk: (1) does a fast, finance-qualified WhatsApp lead convert ≥ ~8% (ideally ~20%)? (2) will dealers/brands actually pay, and how much? (3) can you land a lender pre-approval partnership? The plan is built to falsify these cheaply, fast:

R1 · Wk 1–3

Demand interviews

15–20 dealers/brands/lenders. Extract the real price ceiling: "% walk-in vs paid? run your own ads at what cost? pay marketplaces what per lead?"

R2 · Wk 3–9

Concierge MVP

One city, one brand, ~$1–2k ads, hand-qualify over WhatsApp, route to 3–5 dealers. Measure real lead→sale conversion + time-to-close cohorts.

R3 · Wk 9–13

Invoice for real money

Charge the dealers/brands who got free leads, at 2–3 price points inside the modeled band. Measure free→paid conversion + a lender pre-approval pilot.

Hard kill-gates: G1 ≥30% of interviewees name an in-band price; G2 conversion ≥4% (ideally ≥8%) on ≥150 leads; G3 ≥3 paying customers at a positive contribution margin. Full 90-day plan + gates →

The call

Recommendation & decision
Conditional go — run the test, don't build yet. The market shape, the wave, and the three-payer monetization are strong enough to justify a cheap, time-boxed experiment — but not to justify building product.
