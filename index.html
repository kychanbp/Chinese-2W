Bottom line up front

A real, differentiated structure — the business case is not yet modeled, and two empirical cruxes remain
Verdict

The model is a capital-light, multi-FI motorcycle-finance aggregator in Galgo-free Brazil — you don't lend and you don't just sell leads; you match each buyer across many lenders, earn the origination, and bring a finance-ready customer to the dealer. The desk research validates the structure: the market, the lender supply, the legal vehicle (correspondente), and a proven global blueprint. The P&L is now modeled (2026-06-10): at a 1.7% commission the rejected-buyer wedge is negative per loja (−R$118/mo) — viability requires commission ≥~2.5–3% (a Phase-0 contract read) and converting lojas to whole-flow F&I (+R$372/loja/mo; 31 lojas cover one operator). The remaining unknowns — panel approval on rejects, the entrada, cheap acquisition — are what Phase-1 answers.

The bet — how we win

Ride a growing finance-led market with a structurally better shape

Chinese two-wheelers are flooding LatAm on an accelerating wave (2024 China motorcycle exports $14.5B, +25% — ~36.75M units counting all 2W incl. e-bikes; LatAm #1 destination by value and among the fastest-growing). Underneath, the financing is where the money and the growth are: Brazil moto financing grew +10–18% in 2025 while cars stalled, and Honda’s finance arm captured 47% of its sales — but ~32pp of that is consórcio and only ~15pp CDC loans, so the loan pool is real yet smaller than the headline. New motos dominate financing volume (B3 Nov 2025: 116k new vs 46k used/mo); used is the underserved minority — the white space Omni anchors.

The winning move in one line: be the capital-light, multi-FI moto-finance front-end — soft-pull pre-qualify each buyer across an open lender panel, bring the finance-ready buyer to a weak-financing dealer, and earn the origination — in Galgo-free Brazil, acquired via dealer-QR + TikTok.

Slide 1

The playing field — a financing-led, thin-file, dealer-led market
The market
Brazil ~2.2M motos/yr (+17%); demand is consumer/financing-driven (Mexico/Colombia ~99% "particular"; Honda’s finance arm takes 47% of its Brazil sales — ~32pp consórcio + ~15pp CDC loans). The buyer is young, gig-working, low-income, thin-file — the segment single banks reject.
The brands
Concentrated, not fragmented: Honda 67% of Brazil, Italika 69% of Mexico — non-customers. The opportunity is the challenger tail (CFMoto, Bajaj, Hero) and the financing layer, not displacing the leaders.
The lenders (your supply)
Deep & open — moto finance is dealer-led, so lenders compete via open correspondente programs. The targeted R1 panel (Slide 5): Omni (thin-file anchor) · Banco PAN (#1 ~33%) · BV · Santander · a consórcio — full risk spectrum.
The dealers (your channel)
Dealers already pay for leads (Webmotors per-lead; moto lead-CRMs). Your ICP = small, weak-financing lojas (poor approval, no panel) — for whom you add sales; not strong-F&I concessionárias.

Two structural facts emerge: (1) nobody bridges demand-gen and credit for moto-purchase — agencies can't pre-approve, marketplaces skip motos, loan-marketplaces skip the dealer; (2) the one player who did integrate (Galgo) isn't in Brazil. The bridge is the white space. (Also: you and a dealer buy from the same Meta ad auction — pure per-lead arbitrage is ~zero, which is why the financing, not the lead, is the product.)

Slide 3

What we bring — a FinanZero broker wearing a Capital-One UX

The model is a deliberate composite of the two proven halves — you don't lend (that's the balance-sheet trap that bled Watu, OTO, and Voltz):

Front-end — from Capital One Auto Navigator
Soft-pull pre-qualification: the buyer sees a real, personalized "your R$X/month" on each bike before any hard pull — then browses. Conversion pulled to the top of the funnel.
Back-end — from FinanZero
Multi-FI marketplace via correspondente bancário — capital-light, no balance sheet, commission per disbursal. One application → best-fit lender across an open panel.
The "empty middle" insight. Selling finance-qualified leads is a dominated strategy — once you can credit-approve, keeping the customer and earning the loan beats selling the lead. So the product is the origination; the dealer hand-off is distribution. (Models to copy: Rupyy engine + FinanZero license + Capital One UX — see the comparables teardown.)

Slide 4

What's around the corner — the threats to underwrite
  • 🚨 Galgo enters Brazil, or Mottu pivots outward. Galgo is the integrated model already built (MX/CO/CL/PE); Mottu owns demand+credit+sale for captive delivery riders in Brazil and is ~1 pivot from going broad. The lane is open today — speed matters.
  • 🚨 Meta commoditizes qualification. Meta's native AI agent (live June 2026, tested in Mexico) qualifies leads at the WhatsApp rail. Defense: own generation (TikTok audience) + financing-match, not the qualify-the-chat middle.
  • Regulatory. LGPD enforcement (consent for lead-sharing), correspondente bancário rules, Mexico's China moto tariff (final Dec-2025 decree: 25%; 35% was the proposal) reshaping which brands have budgets.
  • OEM captives (Banco Honda) own their own buyers — compete only for the independent / challenger-brand tail.

Why it beats the integrators & resolves the dealer conflict. As an aggregator you out-approve a single balance sheet (waterfall), scale capital-light, and stay risk-off — defensible as the #1 neutral moto-finance front-end in Galgo-free Brazil. The dealer-commission conflict dissolves by winning on the buyers the dealer's bank rejects: those are incremental sales for the dealer (unit margin they'd have lost) + uncontested origination for you — you grow the pie, you don't split it. (Positioning fork: disintermediate = you're the correspondente, share commission back; enable = dealer stays correspondente, you're the tech layer — no conflict, thinner margin.)

Candor — face reality

What could kill it
Be honest about the risks — none are hidden:
  • The crux is unproven. The entire bet rides on acquiring financeable buyers cheaply (dealer-QR + TikTok). No desk can settle it — it's the Phase-1 test.
  • The earlier, simpler version doesn't pay. Asset-light dealer-lead-selling was marginal-to-negative (~−$0.10/lead at realistic CAC) — which is precisely why the model is the financing aggregator, not a lead vendor. Don't drift back to selling leads.
  • Galgo into Brazil / Mottu pivot would compress the lane fast — this is a speed game.
  • TikTok is a hit-or-miss capability, not a switch; needs sustained, genuinely good content (and a content skill on the team).
  • The P&L is modeled — and the wedge alone doesn’t pay. Revenue = R$184/funded loan (1.7% × R$12k, 10% clawback); wedge-only contribution is −R$118/loja/mo (ops on every reject, commission on ~1 in 8). The business is wedge-to-whole-flow: convert the loja to full F&I (+R$372/loja/mo at a 50/50 split; 31 lojas cover one operator, ~261 reach $500k/yr gross). Existence condition: blended commission ≥~2.5–3% — readable from the contracts in Phase-0.
  • The "white space" has an adverse-selection explanation. The corpus itself derives why incumbents skip moto pre-approval: tiny tickets, same underwriting cost, worse collateral. FinanZero keeping moto CDC thin despite 70+ FI partners is revealed preference. Answer "why does this clear positive ROI for us when it cleared negative for everyone cheaper?" before building.
  • Thin-file alt-data underwriting is the unproven adaptation — Capital One's soft-pull presupposes a thick bureau file; the Brazil version must approve thin-file buyers with alternative data, or the funnel-top is empty.

The test

A focused Phase-1 — prove the one unknown before building

Gate 0 (before any spend): a named, committed PT-BR local operator + a CNPJ path — every Phase-1 task is in Portuguese, on the ground. Then a Phase-0 sprint — desk portion done 2026-06-10 (multi-FI legal under CMN 4.935; third-party-at-POS has 3 routes; retorno 2–6% direct vs 0.75–1.5% promotora sub-table — role determines viability): what remains is the live PT-BR outreach (the 7-question pack per lender) + WhatsApp-interviewing 10–15 weak lojas on rejection rates. Only then spend on the field:

R1 · Wk 2–8 · supply

Panel the targeted lenders

Credential across the risk spectrum — Omni (thin-file anchor) → Banco PAN (#1, subprime) → BV (used) → Santander (prime) → a consórcio (fallback); FBB-130 cert is days, but per-bank credenciamento of a no-storefront broker is weeks–months — use a promotora/master-correspondente if direct stalls. ICE-first. Gate: ≥2 lenders accepting submissions by wk 8.

R2 · Wk 4–14 · acquisition

Dealer-QR concierge pilot

Manual waterfall (re-key fichas into lender portals; build nothing) at 8–12 weak-financing lojas — 3–5 is statistically underpowered. TikTok runs parallel as $0-paid organic only (paid traffic is already ruled out by arithmetic). Gates, on n≥100 dealer-rejected applicants: panel approval ≥25% · approved→funded ≥50% (the entrada survives) · cost per funded loan < commission per loan.

R3 · Wk 12–18 · money

Earn a real commission

Originate real loans and collect the correspondente commission — clawback-tested, cash in the account. Gates: ≥20 funded loans · ≥3 lojas request continuation unprompted · the scale model (loans/loja/mo × BD capacity, + insurance attach) clears a stated revenue floor.

Decision: run the ~$4–6k / ~120-day test (with a local partner’s CNPJ; $8–15k without). Don't build product before R2 proves cheap buyer-acquisition + the approval-uplift conversion. The levers that make it work — multi-FI waterfall, alt-data prequal, dealer-QR + TikTok, weak-financing ICP, Galgo-free Brazil — are all in place; the field numbers — acquisition cost + thin-file approval rate — are what's missing.
