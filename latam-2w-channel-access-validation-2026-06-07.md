# Channel-Access Validation (2026-06-07)

**Question:** do the target challenger brands and the financial institutions have *public programs* a lead-gen / demand-gen intermediary can plug into? **Answer: the two monetization layers validate very differently — the loan layer is turnkey and public; the brand layer requires direct BD.**

## 🟢 Loan / financing layer — STRONGLY validated (public, ready-to-join)

The highest-margin layer ("the moat") has live public programs in all three markets:

| Mechanism | Examples | Offer | Barrier |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Per-loan referral (lowest barrier)** | **Galgo MX $1,000 MXN/moto loan**; **Galgo CO / CrediOrbe $60,000 COP/loan** | Paid per *disbursed* moto loan; self-register, unique link, share via WhatsApp | **None — no legal entity** |
| **Correspondente bancário (BR)** | Banco PAN, BV, Omni, Bradesco Financiamentos, Crefisa | Origination commission, market norm ~up to 6% upfront | CNPJ + FEBRABAN/ANEPS cert |
| **Consórcio rep / affiliate (BR)** | Rodobens 2.2–4%, Porto 0.9%, Mycon ~1% (pure online), Ademicon, Embracon, Servopa | Commission per moto quota sold | Low (Mycon = online affiliate) |
| **Merchant / aliado POS-financing** | Coppel Pay, Sufi (Bancolombia), Addi, Sistecrédito, Brilla, Banco Azteca corresponsal | Earn on financed sales | Business entity, onboarding |
| **Insurance affiliate** | Click Seguros (10–100%), GoAgentes (10% auto), SBS (CO) | Commission on moto policies | Online registration |
| **Fintech partner** | Creditas Partners (perf. commission) + "Indique e Ganhe" (R$50/loan) | Auto/secured-loan commission | Form |

**Standout: Galgo's public referral programs** ($1,000 MXN / $60,000 COP per disbursed moto loan, self-serve, no entity) are a turnkey proof that "send financeable moto buyers → get paid per loan" is a real, accessible business *today*.

## 🟡 Brand / demand-gen layer — WEAKLY validated (BD required, no plug-in)

- All target brands run **dealer-recruitment** funnels ("become a distributor"): CFMoto (Únete a nuestra red), Zontes (¿Quieres ser distribuidor?), QJ Motor (Distribución), Kove (Quiero ser distribuidor), Voge (Torne-se um Concessionário), Hero ("Quiero tener una tienda Hero" — templated across LatAm), TVS (Potential Dealer / MotoMex), **Shineray (Seja um revendedor — still recruiting despite 438 own stores)**.
- **None publish an affiliate / lead-buying / marketing-agency program.** The dealer-recruitment forms are the only public "door," and they recruit *dealers*, not *lead suppliers*.
- **Bajaj-CO has no public door** — gated behind master distributor Grupo UMA.
- **Lever:** QJ Motor + Kove (MX) share importer **Amililla Group** (marketing@amilillagroup.com) — one BD relationship opens two brands.
- → Brand-retainer revenue is a **direct sales effort**, not a program. Higher friction than the thesis assumed; the dealer-recruitment angle (supply would-be-dealer or consumer leads to brands filling networks) is the most natural pitch.

## Strategic implication — re-sequence the beachhead

Financing was framed as "monetize the buyer a *second* time," but it's the **most accessible, turnkey, highest-margin** layer — the *only* one with public self-serve programs. So the lowest-friction entry is as a **moto-loan referral affiliate**: generate financeable buyers → refer to Galgo / CrediOrbe / a consórcio (Mycon) → get paid per disbursed loan. This needs **no BD, no dealer deals, no entity**, and directly tests the core funnel question (can you generate financeable buyers cheaply?) before pitching any brand or dealer. The dealer-fee and brand-retainer layers — both BD-gated — layer on *after* the funnel is proven.

## Caveats

Programs verified on official sites (high confidence on *existence*); commission %s are market-norm/secondary (medium). Galgo referral payouts are in points apps (DCanje/Apprecio) — confirm cash-equivalence. Correspondente bancário requires certification + a CNPJ (and LGPD-compliant data-sharing consent). "No public affiliate program" ≠ "won't deal" — brands may onboard lead partners via direct BD.
