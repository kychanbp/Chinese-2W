# Phase-0 Commission Read — what the desk could get (2026-06-10)

The P&L's existence condition was **blended commission ≥~2.5–3%**. The desk read says: **plausible — IF you are the correspondente of record with your own retorno table (market range 2–6%, regulatory cap 6% upfront); implausible if you enter as an agent under a promotora (published sub-table comps 0.75–1.5%).** The decisive variable is no longer "what does the market pay" but **which role your CNPJ gets**. Exact per-bank tables are confirmed contract-only; the outreach pack below is what extracts them — and it needs the Gate-0 PT-BR operator.

## 1 · Commission benchmark (the P&L input)

| Source | Number | Role it applies to |
|---|---|---|
| **CMN Res. 4.935/2021 art. 15 IV** | upfront commission ≤ **6%** of financed value (≤3% portability); remainder pro-rata over the loan | regulatory ceiling (OFFICIAL) |
| Industry retorno practice | **2–6%** of financed value, via "R-table" tiers (~1–1.5%/step) | loja/correspondente of record |
| Banco PAN (reported anchor) | **~1.5%** of financed value | correspondente (single source, not moto-specific) |
| BV (illustrative, third-party guide) | **~6%** | correspondente |
| Dryve platform agent tiers | **0.75–1.5%** | agent under a platform — the cautionary comp |
| FEBRABAN consignado analogue | clawback if churned ≤360d / re-margined ≤90d | estorno norm reference |

**The retorno system in one breath:** banks hand each loja a menu of rate tables (R0→R10); the higher the client rate the originator closes at, the bigger the commission — top tables historically cost the borrower up to ~14% more over the loan. Legal, but must show in the CET.

**Two strategic consequences:**
1. **Role determines viability.** Correspondente-of-record at 2–6% clears the P&L's ≥2.5–3% bar — Omni's subprime niche (high client rates) has the most retorno headroom. Promotora sub-tables (0.75–1.5%) sit *below* the bar: the promotora fast-path is an onboarding shortcut, not a place to stay.
2. **Retorno conflicts with the neutral-aggregator pitch.** The mechanism pays the originator *more* for selling the buyer a *worse* rate — the exact opposite of "we shop your best rate." Mitigant: art. 14 IV *obliges* a multi-FI correspondente to show each FI's costs side-by-side — the comparison UX is literally a compliance requirement. The venture must price its trust positioning (lower R-tables, volume over margin) consciously.

## 2 · The legal chassis (CMN Res. 4.935/2021) — all questions settled

- **Multi-FI: no exclusivity exists in law.** Art. 14 IV explicitly regulates correspondents acting for multiple FIs (side-by-side cost disclosure required). Practice confirms: moto lojas publicly run 3–6 financeiras (Marello Motos/BH lists PAN+Bradesco+BV+Omni+Safra+Santander; Moto Clube Honda lists Honda/PAN/BV/Santander); Creditas is simultaneously correspondente of Santander/Itaú/Porto/Bradesco across 3,000+ lojas. Only FX cash work requires exclusivity.
- **Third-party-at-POS: legal, three routes.** (1) your CNPJ as direct correspondente; (2) **substabelecimento** — one level of sub-delegation, allowed if the master contract provides and the bank consents (art. 10); (3) contracted by the loja-correspondente. Requirements: the correspondent of record does the atendimento, each proposal carries a **certified attendant's CPF** (art. 15 II), so a third-party originator needs its own certified person on site.
- **The loja cannot be cut out of the money flow:** the bank releases funds **to the selling store** (art. 15 III) — the store issues the nota fiscal and anchors the alienação fiduciária. Strengthens the *enable* positioning; pure disintermediation was never on the table.
- **Favorable quirk:** banks may demand coobrigação (co-liability) only from correspondents financing goods *they themselves sell* (art. 14 IX) — a third-party broker cannot be made guarantor.
- **Estorno:** regulation only mandates that the pro-rata tail stops on early settlement; **upfront clawback is purely contractual** (cancellation/fraud → estorno is standard; windows per bank are contract-only).
- **Certification:** FBB-130 (veículos/CDC): ~R$107, 30 questions, ≥70%, valid 4 years, bookable in days–weeks. FBB-100 (completo): ~R$125, 3 years. Attaches to the *person* attending; ANEPS accepted.

## 4 · What this does to the P&L

Re-run of the existence condition: at **3% retorno** on R$12k the funded loan pays **R$324** (vs R$184 at 1.7%) — wedge-only per-loan margin turns positive at ~16% panel approval (vs ~28%), and whole-flow at 31→~15 lojas per operator equivalent. At **promotora sub-table 1%**: R$108/loan — dead. **The venture's first negotiation is therefore not with lojas but with the banks' regional managers — for a direct code and the table grid.** The rate-linked retorno also adds an honest tension to model in R2: quoting buyers higher-R tables improves your economics but damages conversion and the trust pitch.

## 5 · Outreach pack (the contract-only extraction — needs the PT-BR operator)

**Channels, in order of expected yield:**
1. **BV Coban** — parceiro form + follow the regional gerente de relacionamento; non-store PJ explicitly in scope.
2. **Omni regional Agente** — find via `omni.com.br/encontre-um-agente` for the pilot city; ask for lojista credenciamento + substabelecimento terms.
3. **PAN regional manager** — the named `@grupopan.com` regional emails; ask whether moto credenciamento is open in the pilot region at all.
4. **Bevi / Solopromotora** — sub-credential quote as the speed benchmark.

**The seven questions (per lender, PT-BR):**
1. "Qual é a tabela de retorno para CDC de motos — percentuais por tabela (R0, R1, R2…), bônus por volume e prazo de pagamento da comissão?"
2. "Qual a regra de estorno — liquidação antecipada (janela e proporcionalidade), inadimplência nas primeiras parcelas, fraude?"
3. "Um CNPJ que não é a loja vendedora pode ser credenciado como correspondente e originar propostas dentro de lojas parceiras? Há substabelecimento (Res. 4.935 art. 10)?"
4. "Quais os requisitos de credenciamento — idade do CNPJ, CNAE, vistoria de ponto físico, produção mínima mensal para manter o código ativo?"
5. "A proposta pode ser preenchida digitalmente pelo próprio cliente (auto-atendimento) sob o nosso código, ou exige digitação por atendente certificado?" *(the R$26/app lever)*
6. "Há alguma cláusula de exclusividade ou tabela preferencial condicionada a share de produção?"
7. "Certificação exigida (FBB-100/130/ANEPS) — por atendente ou por ponto? Quem arca?"
